Problem Set: assignment1

Hipster Chips

A logic puzzle by Brandon Bennett

Your problem is to use first-order logic to represent the following example of reasoning, and use the Prover9 theorem prover to prove that the reasoning is valid.

Use the following template file to create your Prover9 encoding of the problem: hipster_chips.p9

Marks: There are two marks for each sentence, making a total of 32 marks.

Outline: Can you represent the following sentences and proove by a chain of irrefutable logical inferences that, in certain circumstances, wearing earhook headphones necessarily entails eating chips?

Problem Statement

A1 Sam and Tam are students who both wear earhook headphones. *
A2 Sam has long hair and does not like sport.
A3 Sporty students don't have long hair.
A4 Hipsters only like tunes that no non-hipster likes.
A5 Hispsters either have long hair or short hair.
A6 Students only whistle tunes they like.
A7 Sam and Tam both whistle the same tune.
A8 Only hipsters and sporty students wear earhook headphones.
A9 All long-haired hipsters are vegetarians.
A10 All hipsters with short hair are allergic to something.
A11 Tam is not allergic to anything.
A12 Vegetarians don't eat food that contains meat. **
A13 All snacks from burger vans contain meat except chips.
A14 Tam is eating a snack from a burger van.
A15 Give a general axiom linking the concepts 'snack' and 'food'. ***
Goal Tam is eating chips.

Non-Logical Vocabulary

Name Constants: sam, tam

Property Predicates: Student, WearsEarhooks, LongHair, ShortHair, Sporty, Hipster, Tune, Vegetarian, Food, Meat, Snack, Chips, BurgerVan

Relation Predicates: Likes, Whistles, Eats, AllergicTo, Contains, From

Notes:

  • * Here we are representing the phrase 'wears earhooks' by a simple property predicate WearsEarhooks. We could break it down into a relation Wears and a property Earhook but that is not necessary to representing the chain of reasoning required to prove the goal.
  • ** Words such as 'food' and 'meat' often refer to substances (types of matter) rather than being properties of individual things. But in the context of this problem we treat them as ordinary predicates. Hence, Food and Meat correspond to properties of being an item of food or a quantity of meat. Similarly, Chips refers to the property of being a portion of chips (not to the totality of all chips in the universe).
  • *** For A15 you should not translate the sentence. As instructed, you should give a formula that captures a simple connection between the concepts 'snack' and 'food' that is required to complete the chain of inference.
