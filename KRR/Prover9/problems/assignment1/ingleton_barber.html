Problem Set: assignment1

The Ingleton Barber

A logic puzzle by Brandon Bennett

Your problem is to use first-order logic (with equality) to represent the following example of reasoning, and use the Prover9 theorem prover to prove that the reasoning is valid.

Use the following template file to create your Prover9 encoding of the problem: ingleton_barber.p9

Marks: There are two marks for each sentence, making a total of 12 marks.

Outline: This puzzle concerns an inconsistent barber from Ingleton and a pink dog that ate my homework.
Ingleton is a village in the Yorkshire Dales, famous for its beautiful waterfalls walk. But if you want to get your hair cut in Ingleton you will end up in tangles.*
You may be surprised that logical reasoning can lead to unexpected consequences.

Problem Statement

A1 Two of the people who live in the village Ingleton are Tom and his old father, Alfred, who is blind.
A2 The concept of 'barber' can be defined as: "any person who shaves some other person". **
A3 No barber is bearded and no person is shaved by a blind person, so Tom shaves Alfred.***
A4 Everyone who lives in a village has a beard, unless they shave themself or are shaved by a barber who lives in the same village. ****
A5 No more than one barber lives in any village and everyone living in Ingleton who shaves themself never gets shaved by a barber.
Goal A pink dog ate my homework. *****

Non-Logical Vocabulary

Constants: tom, alfred, ingleton, me

Property Predicates: Person, Village, Old, Blind, Barber, Bearded, Pink, Dog, Homework

Relation Predicates: Lives, IsFatherOf, Shaves, Ate, Of

Equality relation: =

  • **** This statement leaves open the possibility that in some villages there may be people who shave themselves and also sometimes get shaved by a barber. It also allows the possibility that people with beards may sometimes be shaved.
